Course Overview

This course introduces fundamental concepts, techniques, and tools of data analysis for business management and decision-making. It walks students through basic steps of data analysis, from exploring and describing data to making inferences and predictions applying simulation and inferential statistics. The course explores a variety of methods and topics, including data mining, regression models and optimization analytics.

Learning Objectives In this course, students will:

  • Understand concepts and roles of data analysis in business applications

  • Acquire techniques for describing, analyzing and interpreting data for business decision making

  • Learn how to apply software for data analysis, including Excel and Tableau.

Capstone Project (Group Work) (40%)

The purpose of this project is for you and your group to show off your technical skills, but more importantly for you to begin putting together a portfolio that shows your ability to effectively communicate technical results.

For this project, you and your group members will be creating a blog post and an optional Github repository to begin building a business analysis portfolio of your own.

All data analysis files need to be submitted through CANVAS.

Here is an overview of the main steps in this project, following the CRISP-DM process:

  • Come up with questions your group is interested in answering.

  • Extract the necessary data to answer these questions.

  • Perform necessary cleaning, analysis, and modeling.

  • Evaluate your results.

  • Share your insights with stakeholders in a blog post.

Please check the guideline for more details.

Deliverables:

  1. A blog post on Medium website (sign up) (15%)

  2. 10-min presentation (10%)

  3. Data analysis (code/excel) (15%)
