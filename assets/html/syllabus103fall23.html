Course Objective:

This course is designed to teach students the nature and availability of the financial data and technology tools available at Stevens Institute of Technology, introductory concepts of financial markets, and other relevant tools in financial practice. The focus will be on equity, commodities, foreign exchange (FX), fixed income, and linear and/or nonlinear derivatives (futures, options, swaps, forward rate agreements, etc.) Students will learn how to use the Bloomberg terminal, and as part of the course, they will get a Bloomberg certification. The course also introduces the fundamentals of applied statistics.

Credit hour: 1

The lecturer reserves the right to make adjustments to the course material as necessary.

Outcomes:

  1. After taking this course, students will be able to:

  2. Understand the general concepts of financial markets, firms’ financial data, and economic indicators.

  3. Navigate the available data using the Bloomberg terminal.

  4. Retrieve relevant information from the Bloomberg terminal for their projects in Excel or in R Studio.

  5. Perform simple data analysis on data set, basic statistics concepts.

  6. Analyzing a company's bond or stock and recommending for short/long position.

  7. Construct and analyze their portfolios.

Disability Services Confidentiality Policy

Student Disability Files are kept separate from academic files and are stored in a secure location within the Office of Disability Services. The Family Educational Rights Privacy Act (FERPA, 20 U.S.C. 1232g; 34CFR, Part 99) regulates disclosure of disability documentation and records maintained by Stevens Disability Services. According to this act, prior written consent by the student is required before our Disability Services office may release disability documentation or records to anyone. An exception is made in unusual circumstances, such as the case of health and safety emergencies.
