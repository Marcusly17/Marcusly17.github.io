Working Paper

Overconfident CEOs, Perceived Undervaluation of Stocks and Issuance of New Equities

Coauthored with Suman Banerjee Conference: EFMA2023, FMA2023

Abstract: The study highlights a distinct tendency among overconfident CEOs to prefer issuing new equities over their non-overconfident counterparts. Notably, seasoned equity offerings (SEOs) conducted by firms led by overconfident CEOs generate higher proceeds. The primary motivation behind equity issuance is to sustain investment and acquisition activities, rather than accumulating cash reserves. Consequently, companies with overconfident CEOs witness an uptick in research and development (R&D), property, plant, and equipment (PP&E), as well as capital expenditure subsequent to the equity issuance. There is no evidence of market timing strategies being employed in their equity issuance behavior. Crucially, this increased equity issuance exerts a positive impact on firm performance.

Analyzing the Corporate Diversification Strategy in the Era of Communication Revolution

Solo Paper

Abstract: Our study focuses on investigating how substantial advancements in communication technology impact the diversification strategies of corporations. Enhanced video and audio technologies enable outsourcing firms to communicate with their clients more effectively and efficiently, bridging geographical and time zone gaps, thereby fostering improved collaboration. We propose the hypothesis that these communication advancements will result in companies reducing their reliance on in-house production of inputs, i.e., moving away from vertical integration. Through an extensive analysis encompassing 10,640 companies spanning from 1998 to 2019, we have discovered a notable trend: as communication technology advances, companies tend to reduce their levels of both vertical integration and diversification of business segments. Improved communication can enable companies to focus on their core competencies and outsource non-core functions to specialized service providers. This specialization can lead to a more streamlined and efficient operation, reducing the need for diversification into unrelated business segments. Furthermore, our research delves into the intricate relationships between advancements in communication technology and a range of corporate strategies like investments in research and development (R&D), intangible assets, and the presence of young CEOs. Our findings suggest that improved communication technology acts to alleviate the influence of these three factors on the organizational strategies of a company. Upon analyzing the Management Discussion and Analysis (MDA) sections of companies' 10K filings, we observe that as communication technology advances, firms are demonstrating less explicit interest in technology adoption and outsourcing.

Work in Progress

Differential Sentiments and M&A Performance

Coauthored with Suman Banerjee

Abstract: This research delves into the delicate relationship between the sentiment expressed by company management and the sentiments found in media coverage regarding how these sentiments influence a company's decisions and actions in the realm of Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A). To investigate this connection, we harnessed the power of the Loughran McDonald Dictionary, allowing us to perform a word-level sentiment analysis. Additionally, we leveraged the advanced capabilities of the Financial BERT model to undertake a comprehensive assessment of sentence-level sentiments. The utilization of the Financial BERT model allowed us to conduct an in-depth exploration of deeper sentiments found in news articles, financial reports, and other media sources. Simultaneous use of these advanced model helped us gain a nuanced understanding of the broader sentiment context surrounding a company's M&A activities. According to our initial results, companies that exhibited a combination of pessimistic management sentiments in the preceding year and positive news sentiments in the month prior are more inclined to participate in M&A activities when contrasted with firms within the same industry and possessing a similar market capitalization. Taking a closer look at sentence-level sentiments reveals a clear trend: acquirers who displays pessimistic sentiments over the past year while concurrently having positive news sentiments from the preceding month are at an elevated risk of experiencing significant losses in their M&A ventures. This study emphasizes the crucial importance of sentiment analysis in forecasting the results of M&A and provides valuable insights that can benefit stakeholders in the financial sector.

Teaching

QF-130 Introduction to Financial Tools and Technology

Undergraduate Level (Syllabus) Fall 2023, Spring 2023, Fall 2021

Course Objective: This course is designed to teach students the nature and availability of the financial data and technology tools available at Stevens Institute of Technology, introductory concepts of financial markets, and other relevant tools in financial practice. The focus will be on equity, commodities, foreign exchange (FX), fixed income, and linear and/or nonlinear derivatives (futures, options, swaps, forward rate agreements, etc.) Students will learn how to use the Bloomberg terminal, and as part of the course, they will get a Bloomberg certification. The course also introduces the fundamentals of applied statistics.

BIA-500 Business Analytics: Data, Models & Decisions

Graduate/MBA Level (Syllabus) Fall 2023, Fall 2022

Course Objective: This course introduces fundamental concepts, techniques, and tools of data analysis for business management and decision-making. It walks students through basic steps of data analysis, from exploring and describing data to making inferences and predictions applying simulation and inferential statistics. The course explores a variety of methods and topics, including data mining, regression models, and optimization analytics. Learning Objectives: In this course, students will understand concepts and roles of data analysis in business applications, acquire techniques for describing, analyzing, and interpreting data for business decision-making, and learn how to apply software for data analysis, including Excel and Tableau.
